  against an external sanity reference is well-defined.

  fixture exercises the active-DST timezone path.  The local 18:04
  resolves to 16:04 UTC.
- The latitude is mid-northern, well clear of polar latitudes where
  Placidus house cusps degenerate.

* 5. Cross-check notes
*The cross-checks below are sanity checks, not the oracle.*  Per
D26 the canon — not the external reference — is the authority.

** Astrology
- The published Schiedam reference chart that has been the working
  PoC sanity reference all along reports ascendant *Virgo 25°06'*
  and MC *Gemini 23°35'*.
- The frozen baseline reports ascendant *175.114625°* (= Virgo
  25°06.88') and MC *83.606577°* (= Gemini 23°36.39').
- Match to within ~1 arc-minute on each angle, which is consistent
  with the published chart's degree-and-minute precision combined
  with Swiss Ephemeris 2.10.03 vs whatever ephemeris the published
  chart was originally computed with.

** Human Design — algebraic re-anchoring against the PoC
- The pre-Trinity PoC golden case used a different gate sequence
  anchor (313.25°) and a different =GateOrder=.  The two systems
  emit different gate numbers but must agree on the *underlying
  ecliptic longitude*.
- Spot check at the Sun: PoC =personality_sun = 51.5= under the
  313.25° anchor corresponds to the same Sun longitude
  (≈ 19.540°) under the canon's 277.5° anchor =GateOrder=, which
  the canon-compliant engine maps to =42.1=.
- The frozen baseline's =personality_sun= = =42.1=.  Match.

** Design-time
- The design-time bisection produces =1990-01-12T00:38:22Z= under
  the corrected D22 lower-bound + truncation rule.  Sanity
  envelope: birth UTC is =1990-04-09T16:04Z=, so the design moment
  sits ≈ 87.65 days earlier than birth, consistent with the
  perihelion-accelerated solar rate (~1.004°/day in early January)
  needed to traverse the canonical 88° offset.

** Source-stack pins
- =/version= reports =swisseph_version: 2.10.03= and =tzdb_version:
  2026a=, identical to canon Document 03 pins.  The =+VERSION=
  marker shipped in the production image confirms the runtime tzdb
  matches the canon release.

* 6. Boundary-area review (D26 §"ambiguity prone areas")
D26 calls for "independent review of ambiguity prone areas such as
timezone-sensitive cases, sign boundaries, house boundaries, gate
and line boundaries, Design-time sub-second stop behavior, and
error classification edge cases".  Each is examined below.

** 6.1 Timezone-sensitive case
The fixture sits *after* the 1990 NL spring-DST transition
(2026-03-25), inside the active-DST window.  A regression in tzdb
release pinning would change the local→UTC conversion by one hour;
the canonical baseline uses the canon-pinned IANA 2026a release
(verified at boot via =canon.AssertTZDBVersion= reading
=$ZONEINFO/+VERSION=).

The conversion 18:04 Europe/Amsterdam → 16:04 UTC under 2026a
matches the historical record for that date.  Edge cases the
fixture does *not* exercise are deliberately covered by the other
=valid_edge= fixtures (the equator and arctic-latitude cases under
=src/golden/trinity/valid_edge/=).

** 6.2 Sign boundaries
The 13 astrology objects all sit comfortably mid-sign; the
*tightest* margin to a sign cusp is *Venus 333.397401°* at
*3.397°* from the Aquarius/Pisces cusp (300°→330°→360°: Pisces
spans 330–360, Venus is 3.4° into Pisces).  No body falls within
the 0.5° canon-stress window of any sign cusp.  Full table in
=boundary-distances.txt=.

** 6.3 House boundaries
House cusps follow Document 12 D21's start-inclusive / next-cusp-
exclusive rule.  Tightest margin: *Pluto 227.134°* at *0.644°*
from a house cusp.  No body falls within the 0.5° canon-stress
window; the closest case is comfortably outside it.

** 6.4 Gate boundaries
Canon mandala anchor = 277.5°, gate width = 5.625°.  Tightest
margin: *Pluto* at *0.259°* from a gate cusp.  No body falls
within the 0.1° gate-stress window.  All gate assignments are
therefore robust to small perturbations from any future Swiss
Ephemeris release within the same =SE_VERSION= 2.10.03 family.

** 6.5 Line boundaries
Line width = 0.9375°.  Tightest margin: *Jupiter 93.769635°* at
*0.0196°* from a line cusp.  This is *inside* the 0.02° stress
window I would normally flag.  Worth noting explicitly:

- Jupiter's astrology longitude 93.769635° puts it 0.0196° below
  the next line cusp.  The line assignment in the chart is
  *deliberately* on the lower side of that cusp under the canon's
  start-inclusive / next-cusp-exclusive rule.
- Even an ephemeris drift of ~1 arc-minute (0.017°) would not flip
  the assignment, because the cusp is *above* Jupiter; Jupiter
  would have to rise to or past it.
- The corresponding HD activation, however, derives from gate /
  line at the Sun (=personality_sun= and =design_sun= and friends),
  not at Jupiter's longitude.  Jupiter only appears in the
  astrology block where the assignment is explicit and stable
  under the rule.
- I flag this as the tightest line-boundary observation in the
  baseline so the reviewer can confirm the assignment is the
  intended one.  Full table in =boundary-distances.txt=.

** 6.6 Design-time sub-second stop
A3 / D22 specifies that when the design-time bisection stops
because the remaining interval width is < 1 second, the canonical
Design-time is the *lower bound* of the final search interval,
truncated to whole seconds.  This branch is now protected by an
explicit unit-test sentinel,
=TestSolveDesignTimeSubSecondStopReturnsLowerBound= in
=pkg/hd/calc/designtime_test.go=, that:

- Forces the solver into the bracket-width stop (steep synthetic
  Sun rate so the abs-diff stop cannot fire first).
- Asserts =ExitReason == "bracket_width_threshold"=.
- Asserts =FinalBracketDays < 1 second=.
- Asserts =got == diag.FinalLowerJD= bit-exact (any midpoint or
  upper-bound regression diverges by up to 0.5 / 1 second; the
  sentinel catches both with a precise delta in the failure
  message).
- Has been mutation-tested (the sentinel passes on the canon-
  correct solver, fails on midpoint with delta +0.412 s, fails on
  upper-bound with delta +0.824 s — see commit =55802c3=).

** 6.7 Error classification edge cases
Out of scope for the *baseline* fixture (it is a valid case).
Error classification is exercised by the =invalid_input=,
=incomplete_input=, and =unsupported_input= fixture categories
under =src/golden/trinity/=, which carry their own per-category
expected-error envelopes and their own canon attributions
(D23 for error_type / error_code, Document 04 strict input
contract + D08 minute-precision boundary for invalid vs
unsupported, D24 for IANA-canonical-only timezone identifiers).
